Putnum Museum and Science Center

Groups: contact Alice Loff call 563-336-7296.

The Putnam provides a unique, hands-on experience great for the whole family.The Putnam’s exciting new 10,000-square-foot Science Center features more than 45 hands-on stations that make science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) concepts fun for visitors of all ages.Five Exhibit Halls, plus changing traveling exhibitions, stimulate the imagination. Black Earth/Big River is an interactive display of ecosystems of the Upper Mississippi River Valley; Hall of Mammals lets you see and hear life-like African and Arctic animals in their natural settings; River, Prairie and People explores the history of the Quad Cities region; and the Asian/Egyptian Gallery explores the mysteries of ancient civilizations and includes a 3,000 year-old mummy.Top it off when you experience the National Geographic Giant Screen Theater, towering five stories high and seven stories wide. Admission charged.
Putnum Museum website

John Deere Pavillion

(adjacent to hotel)
Phone: +1 (309) 765-1000
Find surprising, exciting and engaging fun for everyone at the John Deere Pavilion. Experience the innovations of the present and future while appreciating the heritage of the past. See vintage machines and climb aboard new ones. Operate a simulated excavator or dozer and watch feature films. Special events are scheduled throughout the year. And it's all free.
If you think the John Deere Pavilion is essentially a tractor garage, then it’s time for you to discover the history, advanced technology and strong work ethic that have majorly influenced this company that’s more than 170 years old.

Chocolate Manor

563-355-6600
110 E. 2nd St.
Davenport, Iowa
This wonderful shop provides one-hour tours of their artisan, handcrafted chocolate shop. Up to 30 people at a time can enter the kitchen where they can experience hand dipping of seasonal confectionaries and watch chocolates made. In the second half of the tour, you will learn all about chocolate and how chocolate is made. Sampling from their cases is included! Their English Toffee is superb and their soft butter caramel is even better.

Vander Veer Botanical Park

215 W Central Park in Davenport
Since its establishment in 1885, gardens and floral displays have been a tradition at this beautiful 33 acre park, inviting visitors to stroll from the Conservatory to the Stone Fountain. The park grounds are home to an extensive collection of gardens and trees, including many planted during the early 1890's.

Vander Veer Botanical Park website

Channel Cat Water Taxi

The Channel Cat Water Taxi is a leisurely way to get out and enjoy the Mississippi River. The Channel Cats are open-air boats that take passengers back and forth across the Mississippi River to various landings. Passengers can get on an off as they like for one ticket price good for all day usage.
Operates seven days a week, Memorial Day through September. Bicyclists and their bikes are welcome on board the Channel Cat.
All-day tickets:
Adults: $8
Children age 2-10: $4
Children under two accompanied by an adult ride free.

Channel Cat Water Taxi website

Lagomarcino's

At Lagomarcino’s we are not only known for our fine homemade chocolates, we have also been called “The best old-fashioned ice cream parlor in the world.” We feature malts, ice cream sodas, egg cremes and ice cream sundaes. Try our famous hot fudge sundae, a bitter sweet fudge sauce served over home made ice cream. Need something to eat before dessert, we also serve soups, salads and sandwiches. Customer favorites include our baked ham and swiss or egg salad sandwich served on our light Swedish rye bread. You can company it with a Green River or a Lago, a beverage similar to Dr. Pepper developed by Charlie Lagomarcino in the 1920’s. Enjoy all of this seated in one of our mahogany booths or seated at our authentic soda fountain, where you can watch your treats being prepared.
